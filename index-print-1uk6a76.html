The Veterans Crisis Line works for the people who pick up the phone — 1.3 million contacts in FY2025, 97% satisfaction. But getting there takes two things going right, and for the 17.5, at least one of them doesn't.

First, you have to want to dial it — with enough fight left to ask a stranger for help. Second, the voice on the other end has to be someone you can actually talk to. A veteran in crisis knows within ten seconds whether that person ever wore the uniform.

THE SOLUTION

A different system, built around the people who fall through.

01

Issued at discharge

Every veteran leaves the service with a dedicated device — custom-engraved with their career, presented as an honor in a polished wooden box. Not flagged. Not opt-in. The stigma is gone because everyone carries one.

02

A fellow veteran answers

A beacon routes to peers matched by branch, MOS, deployments, and unit. The person on the line stood the same fire watch and came home to the same silence. That familiarity is what keeps a veteran talking.

03

A VA clinician oversees

Every transmission is monitored in real time by licensed VA staff on a 911-style console with the veteran’s full context. Only a clinician — never an automated rule — decides when 911 is dispatched.

THE DEVICE

Issued as an honor.
Carried as a lifeline.

Custom service artwork
Name, rank, branch, deployments, and awards — designed by hand-picked American artists. A device a veteran is proud of is a device that’s still there at 2 a.m.
Three functions, nothing else
Initiate a beacon, receive a transmission, open a secured two-way voice channel. No apps, no feed, no second purpose. A lifeline and nothing else.
It never fails silent
Dual-mode FirstNet cellular and Iridium satellite. If one network drops, the other holds. Works anywhere under the sky.
Identity-gated & private
An SSN or military ID is entered before any transmission activates — ensuring privacy and preventing abuse.
THE CASCADE

What happens the moment a beacon fires.

STEP 01

Beacon

A veteran triggers the device or the app. The lifeline goes live — overriding silent and focus modes.

STEP 02

Top-10 match

The system ranks the ten closest peer responders — by branch, MOS, deployments, awards, and unit history.

STEP 03

A peer answers

A fellow veteran picks up and authenticates. If one can’t be reached, the next is already ringing.

STEP 04

Secured voice

An encrypted two-way channel opens between the veteran in crisis and the responder who answered.

STEP 05

VA console

A licensed clinician monitors in real time — diagnoses, meds, prior crises, safety plan, contacts, all on one screen.

STEP 06

911 — clinician-gated

Only when a clinician confirms the threshold is NG911 / RapidSOS dispatched — with exact GPS and the flags responders need.

The veteran is never asked to repeat the worst day of their life to three operators while the clock runs. Responders don’t walk in blind.
