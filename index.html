WHAT LS‑2025 IS

A lifeline a veteran is proud to carry — wired into a network that actually answers.

LS‑2025 is a dedicated communication device and linked app, issued to every service member at discharge — both a token of appreciation and a permanent connection point. It is engraved with their service and presented in a wooden box, so it is carried with pride, not stigma.

One press opens a secure line to a fellow veteran matched by branch, unit, and deployments — someone who understands. A licensed VA clinician oversees every connection in real time, and only a clinician can escalate to 911. A conversation becomes real help in seconds.

THE CASCADE

What happens the moment a beacon fires.

STEP 01

Beacon

A veteran triggers the device or the app. The lifeline goes live — overriding silent and focus modes.

STEP 02

Top-10 match

The system ranks the ten closest peer responders — by branch, MOS, deployments, awards, and unit history.

STEP 03

A peer answers

A fellow veteran picks up and authenticates. If one can’t be reached, the next is already ringing.

STEP 04

Secured voice

An encrypted two-way channel opens between the veteran in crisis and the responder who answered.

STEP 05

VA console

A licensed clinician monitors in real time — diagnoses, meds, prior crises, safety plan, contacts, all on one screen.

STEP 06

911 — clinician-gated

Only when a clinician confirms the threshold is NG911 / RapidSOS dispatched — with exact GPS and the flags responders need: weapons, intoxication, or threats to others.

The veteran is never asked to repeat the worst day of their life to three operators while the clock runs. Responders don’t walk in blind — and the same data, over time, shows the language and stressors that precede a crisis, so prevention gets sharper.

WHO CARRIES IT

Everyone carries one — so no one is singled out.

A badge of service, not a label of weakness

LS‑2025 is issued to every eligible veteran at discharge — not just those already in crisis. Because everyone carries it, no one can be singled out as “the suicidal vet” for having one.

A veteran at work, on the street, or without a home can carry it without drawing a second look. To anyone watching, it reads as a symbol of service and a way to keep serving — part of a national mission, not a sign of trouble.

Every holder is a responder — and, if they need it, a caller

Veterans are often the best people to reach other veterans. Holders can volunteer for scheduled windows when they stand watch — taking calls from those who match their service background — as part of a distributed national support team.

And the same device is theirs to use if their own footing slips. Volunteering to help others never costs a veteran the right to reach out.

Veterans leave the service still needed. LS‑2025 turns “I’m done” into “I still have a mission” — which itself cuts the isolation that discharge can bring.

The Veterans Crisis Line works for the people who pick up the phone — 1.3 million contacts in FY2025, 97% satisfaction. But getting there takes two things going right, and for the 17.5, at least one of them doesn’t.

First, you have to want to dial it — with enough fight left to ask a stranger for help. Second, the voice on the other end has to be someone you can actually talk to. A veteran in crisis knows within ten seconds whether that person ever wore the uniform. LS‑2025 closes both gaps before the worst night ever comes.

WHERE IT STARTED

From Mission Daybreak to a system that’s built.

In 2022, founder Brandon Bible entered the VA’s Mission Daybreak challenge with a concept he called Veterans Saving Veterans — built on one hard question: what if every veteran left the service with a dedicated radio that could save their life? That first submission held the backbone LS‑2025 still runs on today.

It did not appear last week in a pitch deck. It began as a full written system concept and has been refined ever since — into a veteran-only, 911-style network staffed by veterans and clinicians, with real-time crisis detection, satellite reach “anywhere under the sky,” and custom device artwork. LS‑2025 is the hardened version of that original idea.
